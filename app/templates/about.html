Bamnit is a service for substitute teachers to receive notifications when their preferred jobs become available.

What does this website do?

The Problem: When a school teacher plans to miss a day of school, the school district has to find a temporary replacement (a substitute teacher) to come in and teach the class for the day. Many school districts use a service called AesopOnline, which is essentially just a static webpage that lists a school district's available jobs. Substitute teachers have to continually manually log onto AesopOnline to check for jobs, and then manually scour the list of hundreds of open jobs for those they are interested in.

The Solution:Bamnit is a tool where teachers can specify exactly what kind of jobs they are looking for (e.g. Middle School Art classes, High School Math classes) and receive an email notification when those jobs are available.

In addition to the Notification tool, I've created an API for the website so that users can programmatically interact with the service. I've also created a Dashboard and various graphs to explore the data I've gathered on teacher absences. The available data is specifically from Austin Independent School District.

Why did you build this website?

I created this website for two reasons:

  1. I do occasional part-time work as a substitute teacher (whenever I can manage to rouse myself at 6:00am), and wanted to get quick emails whenever jobs with my favorite grade or subject to teach were available.
  2. My longer-term career is in computer programming, and I built this project as a demonstration of the variety of my skill set. It took several different pieces of technology to bring everything together, and I'm happy with how it turned out.

You can view the source code for this website at https://github.com/zetterlund/Bamnit.

Feel free to create an account, add some notifications, and play around with the tool!
